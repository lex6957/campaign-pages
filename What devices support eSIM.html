List of eSIM-supported devices

If you want to use an eSIM, your device needs to be both carrier-unlocked and compatible with eSIM technology.  Please refer to the list below to check if your device supports eSIM (note that there may be country and carrier-specific restrictions).  

*Important: This list will be updated as new eSIM-compatible devices become available. While we strive to keep the list comprehensive, it may not include every compatible device; your device could still support eSIM even if it is not listed.  

As of February 2025, the devices below support eSIM technology:

*The following devices DO NOT support eSIM:

  • iPhones from Hong Kong and Macao (except for the iPhone 13 Mini, iPhone 12 Mini, iPhone SE 2020, and iPhone XS). 

 

*For iPhones purchased in TURKEY: 

  • For devices set up after June 23, 2020, eSIM activates upon installation. Contact your carrier if it's not enabled. 
  • For devices set up before June 23, 2020, follow the steps below to activate your eSIM. Backup your data, as the process may erase it.  
  1. Back up your device and update to the latest iOS or iPadOS. Go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset > Erase All Content and Settings.  
  1. Enter your passcode or Apple ID password to confirm. After erasing, restore your device from the backup and set up your eSIM. 

Note: To use roaming data services in Turkey for 91 days or more within a 120-day period, your device's IMEI must be registered with Turkey's Central Equipment Identity Register. Contact CEIR for details. Source: https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT211023
